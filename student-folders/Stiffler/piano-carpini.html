John of Plano Carpini

Friar John of Plano Carpini, natively known as Giovanni da Pian del Carpine, was born around the year 1185. A native of Umbria, he became respected within the Franciscan order.

Friar John held numerous positions across Europe, such as warden and provincial. His roles were important in countries including Italy, Germany, and eventually France. While he was serving as a provincial of Germany in 1241, the Mongolian invasion of Europe had reached Poland.

Four years pass until the culmination of Friar John’s journey when Pope Innocent IV convened the friar mission to the Mongols. Friar John carried a letter from Pope Innocent IV to its intended recipient, the ruler of the Mongols.

For our map, we used the Stevenson translation from 2004, though as this is still in copyright, the links from the map will take the user to the appropriate passages in the Jones translation of 1928.
